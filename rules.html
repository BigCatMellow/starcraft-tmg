1. Overview
The shortest possible overview
›

This is a four-phase alternating-activation wargame. Units usually begin off-table in Reserves, deploy gradually as Supply allows, and try to control Mission Markers to score Victory Points.

  • Phase 1Movement: Deploy, Move, Disengage, or Hold.
  • Phase 2Assault: Ranged Attack, Charge, Run, or Hold.
  • Phase 3Combat: Every engaged ground unit that is still engaged must fight.
  • Phase 4Scoring: Determine marker control, score VP, resolve end-of-round effects, refresh cards, and set next round initiative.
💡
2. Core Concepts
Supply, engagement, coherency, cards
›
2.1 Supply ›
  • Current Supply updates as casualties reduce model count.
  • Available Supply = current Supply Pool minus the Supply already on the battlefield.
  • A unit can only deploy from Reserves if its Current Supply fits inside Available Supply.
  • Some missions also score based on enemy Supply destroyed.
2.2 Engagement ›
  • Engaged units can Disengage or Hold in Movement.
  • Engaged units can only target units they are already engaged with in Assault.
  • Engaged ground units that are still engaged in Combat must activate and fight.
  • Flying units never participate in Combat Phase.
2.3 Leading Model & Coherency ›
  • Most movement is leader-first. Move the Leading Model first, then set the rest in legal Coherency.
  • Every model must end wholly within 3 inches of the Leading Model and maintain a legal Coherency Link.
  • The rest of the unit is set after the leader moves; you do not individually walk every model.
  • Casualties do not by themselves trigger a new coherency check later.
2.4 Ready & Exhausted Cards ›
  • Ready = face-up. The card can provide resources or use its own ability.
  • Exhausted = face-down. It provides no resources and cannot use abilities until Cleanup & Refresh.
  • Resources are generated and spent at the moment you exhaust a card. Unused extra output is lost.

3. Round Structure
Initiative and flow
›
Before Round 1 ›
  • Choose the mission, terrain, deployment map, and starting First Player Marker.
  • Your army is bought during roster building, but most units usually begin off-table in Reserves.
  • You do not usually deploy your full army before play starts. Deployment is folded into Movement Phase as Supply escalates.
Start of Each Round ›
  • Resolve Start of the Round effects and any Tactical Card timing.
  • Check the mission Supply Pool for the current round. Many missions increase it each round.
  • Recalculate Available Supply before deploying new units.
Two Common Misunderstandings ›
  • You do not deploy your whole army at game start. Most units enter gradually during Movement as Supply allows.
  • Charging happens in Assault, but close-combat attacks are resolved later in Combat Phase.
1️⃣
Phase 1Movement
Deploy, Move, Disengage, Hold
›

Movement is for bringing units onto the table, repositioning unengaged units, escaping melee, and managing tempo through passing.

You are not just moving models. You are deciding which pieces enter play, which lanes they threaten, and whether you want one more activation or first move in the shooting phase.

Deploy ›
  • Choose a unit in Reserves that has not activated this phase.
  • Its Current Supply must fit inside Available Supply.
  • Place the Leading Model in contact with your Entry Edge, then move it up to Speed using standard movement rules.
  • Set the rest of the unit in legal Coherency.
  • The unit cannot finish deployment inside the enemy Zone of Influence.
Move ›
  • Choose an unengaged on-table unit.
  • Move the Leading Model up to Speed, then set the rest in Coherency.
  • A normal Move cannot end within 1 inch of an enemy ground unit.
  • All standard pathing, terrain, elevation, and gap-clearance rules still apply.
Disengage ›
  • Choose an engaged unit and move it using standard Move rules.
  • Every surviving model must end outside the Engagement Range of all enemy units it was engaged with.
  • Any model that cannot get clear is removed.
  • If the Leading Model cannot get clear, the unit does not move, the leader is removed, and the activation ends.
  • A unit that disengages normally cannot make a Ranged Attack or Charge in the following Assault Phase.
  • That penalty is ignored if, when disengaging, its Current Supply exceeds the combined Supply of all engaged enemies (Tactical Mass).
Hold ›
  • Hold does nothing, but the unit still counts as activated.
  • This matters when you want Assault initiative more than one more weak movement activation.
2️⃣
Phase 2Assault
Ranged Attack, Charge, Run, Hold
›

Assault is where on-table units either shoot, charge, run, or hold. Engagement status quietly invalidates many otherwise legal-looking targets.

Assault is where damage, target legality, and setup for melee all collide. Many turns hinge on whether you take one more attack now or preserve first activation in Combat.

Ranged Attack ›
  • A legal target must be visible, within range, and match the weapon's target tag.
  • An unengaged attacker cannot normally target an engaged enemy unit.
  • An engaged attacker may only target the enemy unit(s) it is already engaged with.
  • Each batch is all models using the same weapon profile at the same target.
  • Resolve one batch completely before declaring the next batch.
  • If a unit has multiple weapon profiles, separate batches can target different enemy units.
Attack Sequence ›
  • 1. Build the Attack Pool.
  • 2. Roll to Hit.
  • 3. Resolve Surge if the target's tags match the weapon's Surge Type.
  • 4. Roll Armour.
  • 5. Roll Evade only if the defender is entitled to it.
  • 6. Apply Damage and remove casualties.
Charge ›
  • Charge is for an unengaged ground unit only.
  • Declare all target units before rolling.
  • Line of sight is not required.
  • Roll 1D6 + Speed for charge distance.
  • The Leading Model must legally end within 1 inch of every declared target, must not overlap bases or impassable terrain, and must not end within 1 inch of an undeclared enemy.
  • If any declared target cannot be reached legally, the charge fails completely and the unit does not move.
Run & Hold ›
  • Run is identical to a standard Move action during Assault.
  • Hold does nothing but still activates the unit.
  • A unit that disengaged in Movement usually cannot shoot or charge, so Run or Hold are often its only Assault choices.
3️⃣
Phase 3Combat
Melee resolution
›

Combat resolves all melee. It is mandatory for any engaged ground unit that is still engaged when its turn to activate comes.

Assault is where you move into melee; Combat is where that melee actually pays off or collapses.

Close Ranks ›
  • Before rolling, choose a Leading Model and move it up to 3 inches using standard Move rules with extra restrictions.
  • The Leading Model must end closer to the enemy unit(s) it is engaged with.
  • The move cannot bring the unit into engagement with new enemy units.
  • The move cannot be used to disengage from enemy units.
  • Models already in base-to-base contact do not move.
  • Close Ranks is mainly for pulling more models into Fighting Rank and Supporting Rank.
Who Can Attack in Melee ›
  • Fighting Rank: models within 1 inch of an enemy model.
  • Supporting Rank: models in base-to-base contact with a friendly model from the same unit that is in Fighting Rank.
  • Line of sight is not required for melee attacks.
  • Use only Combat Phase weapons.
  • If a model has more than one eligible melee weapon, the controlling player chooses one.
Multiple Enemy Units ›
  • If a unit is engaged with more than one enemy unit, declare how attack dice are split before rolling.
  • Declare which target receives the Surge Die before rolling.
  • That declaration is binding.
Combat Reminders ›
  • Melee uses the same six-step attack sequence as ranged attacks.
  • Evade in melee is NOT automatic. A unit only gets it if a rule explicitly grants it.
  • Use the engaged casualty priority rules.
  • After casualties are removed, if none of the unit's models remain within 1 inch of an enemy, the unit becomes unengaged immediately.
  • A unit freed from combat before it activates no longer has to fight that phase.
4️⃣
Phase 4Scoring & Cleanup
VP, markers, refresh
›

This phase decides who is actually winning. Follow the order strictly.

This is where board position becomes score, and where the round-end timing window matters. Do not skip straight from fighting to refreshing cards.

Mission Marker Control ›
  • A unit contests only if it is on the battlefield, in coherency, and has at least one model within 3 inches of the marker with line of sight to it and on the same elevation.
  • Flying units cannot contest or control.
  • Burrowed units cannot contest or control.
  • Control is determined by eligible Current Supply, not model count.
  • A tie does not transfer control.
  • Control is sticky: once a player controls a marker, it stays theirs until the enemy wins a later comparison with higher Supply.
End of Game & Refresh ›
  • The game can end by mission special win condition, tabling, or round limit.
  • If a player has no models on the battlefield and no units in Reserves, the surviving player gains +10 VP.
  • At the start of the final Scoring Phase, units still in Reserves are treated as destroyed for scoring.
  • End-of-round effects resolve before Cleanup & Refresh.
  • Cleanup removes temporary tokens (keeping Damage Markers, Mission Markers, Faction Indicators).
  • Exhausted Tactical Cards and Faction Cards refresh here — not earlier.

5. Situation Guide
What can I do right now?
›
My unit is in Reserves ›
  • It normally cannot use Active, Passive, or Reaction abilities unless a rule explicitly says it can.
  • Its normal action is to Deploy during Movement if its Supply fits.
  • It does not act in Assault or Combat while in Reserves.
My unit is unengaged on the battlefield ›
  • MovementMove or Hold.
  • AssaultRanged Attack, Charge, Run, or Hold.
  • CombatNothing unless it becomes engaged.
  • ScoringContest a marker if it meets all conditions.
My unit is engaged ›
  • MovementDisengage or Hold.
  • AssaultIt can activate, but targeting is heavily restricted.
  • CombatIf it is a ground unit and still engaged, it must fight.
My unit just disengaged ›
  • It normally cannot make a Ranged Attack or Charge in the following Assault Phase.
  • If Tactical Mass applied at disengage, that penalty is ignored.
  • Its realistic Assault options are often Run or Hold.
I want to shoot ›
  • Check visibility, range, and target tags first.
  • Then check engagement legality — engagement can invalidate an otherwise legal-looking target.
  • Declare and resolve batches one at a time.
I want to charge ›
  • The unit must be unengaged and grounded.
  • Declare all targets first.
  • A multi-charge only succeeds if all declared targets can be reached legally.
I am in melee with multiple enemy units ›
  • You must split attack dice before rolling.
  • You must declare which target gets the Surge Die before rolling.
  • You cannot assign results after seeing the dice.
I am near a Mission Marker ›
  • Distance alone is not enough.
  • You need: battlefield presence, coherency, line of sight, same elevation, and correct unit state.
  • Flying, Burrowed, and out-of-coherency units do not contest.
My card is Ready ›
  • It may be exhausted for resources or to use its own ability, depending on timing and cost.
  • That choice is made when you use the card, not preselected for the round.
My card is Exhausted ›
  • It provides no resources and cannot use abilities until Cleanup & Refresh.
  • You cannot double-dip a face-down card for both resources and an ability.

6. Abilities & Resources
Active, Passive, Reaction, Payment
›
Active Abilities ›
  • May only be used while the unit is currently activated.
  • Must be triggered immediately before declaring an action or immediately after one fully resolves.
  • Cannot be used in the middle of an action.
  • Cannot normally be used while the unit is in Reserves.
  • Each named Active ability is usually once per round per unit unless it is Repeatable.
Passive Abilities ›
  • Always on while the unit is on the battlefield.
  • Do not require activation.
  • Do not normally function while in Reserves unless stated otherwise.
Reaction Abilities ›
  • Trigger in direct response to a defined event.
  • Must be declared at the exact trigger moment.
  • Cannot be used retroactively.
  • Each player may resolve only one Reaction per Activation.
  • If both players react to the same trigger, the Active Player resolves first.
Resource Payment Rules ›
  • Terran uses CP, Zerg uses BM, and Protoss uses PE.
  • To pay a cost, exhaust a Ready Faction Card or Tactical Card that produces the required resource type.
  • If you cannot exhaust enough cards to pay the full cost, the ability cannot be activated.
  • Excess output is lost.
  • Resources generated for one ability cannot be saved for another later ability.

7. Common Mistakes
High-frequency errors & judge reminders
›
Walking every model individually instead of moving the Leading Model first and then setting the rest in coherency.
Forgetting that Deploy still uses standard movement rules and can be blocked by Available Supply or Zone of Influence.
Forgetting that disengage can kill trailing models and that the leader dying can cause the whole move to fail.
Letting a disengaged unit shoot or charge in Assault when Tactical Mass did not remove the penalty.
Shooting into melee illegally with an unengaged unit.
Letting an engaged attacker target enemies it is not engaged with.
Declaring all weapon targets at once instead of one batch at a time.
Treating a failed multi-charge as a partial success.
Forgetting Close Ranks in Combat.
Giving melee attacks an Evade Roll automatically.
Failing to split dice before rolling when engaged with multiple enemy units.
Using visibility-based casualty removal when engaged casualty priority is required.
Counting model count instead of Current Supply for Mission Marker control.
Letting Flying, Burrowed, or Out-of-Coherency units contest markers.
Refreshing exhausted cards too early.
